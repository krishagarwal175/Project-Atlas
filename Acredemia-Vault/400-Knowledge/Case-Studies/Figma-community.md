# 📄 Figma — Community & Multiplayer

## Problem
Design tools were single-player and locked to one machine; collaboration was painful.

## Strategy
Browser-based multiplayer design + a Community where users publish, fork, and share files — turning shared artifacts into a distribution and identity loop.

## Execution
Real-time collaboration lowered the cost of inviting others (you invite to *work together*, not just to sign up); Community made others' work visible and remixable, creating identity and belonging.

## Outcome (metrics)
Became the dominant collaborative design tool; community content became a top acquisition channel.

## Why it worked
- Collaboration is intrinsically viral: using the product *requires* pulling others in.
- Community gave **identity**, not just utility — a stronger retention force than incentives.
