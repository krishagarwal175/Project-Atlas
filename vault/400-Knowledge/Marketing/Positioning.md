# 🧰 Positioning

> Positioning is the *context* you set so customers understand what you are, who you're for, and why you're the obvious choice. It's decided before messaging — messaging just expresses it. (April Dunford's framing.)

## What it is
The set of deliberate choices: **competitive alternatives** (what customers would use instead, incl. doing nothing), your **unique attributes**, the **value** those attributes enable, the **customer segment** that cares most, and the **market category** you frame yourself in. Get the frame wrong and even great messaging fails.

## When to use it
Before launch, before any big marketing spend, and whenever prospects "don't get it" or compare you to the wrong things.

## How to run it
1. List real alternatives. 2. Find attributes you have that they don't. 3. Translate attributes → value. 4. Identify who values that most. 5. Pick the category that makes your value obvious. 6. *Then* write messaging.

## Why it works
Customers understand new things by analogy to a category. You either choose the frame or the market chooses it for you (often unfavorably).

## Limitations / when NOT to use it
- Positioning ≠ slogan; a clever tagline on wrong positioning still fails.
- Over-narrow framing can cap the market; revisit as you grow.
