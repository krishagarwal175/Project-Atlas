# 🎯 Pilot in Tier-2 colleges as the beachhead

> Worked example demonstrating the full loop: Question → Evidence → DNA check → Quality score → Experiment → (later) Outcome.

## Decision
Run the first verified-hiring pilot in 2–3 Tier-2 colleges before approaching Tier-1.

## Why — the reasoning trace (the actual asset)
Tier-2 students face sharper credibility gaps with recruiters, so verification's value proposition is *more* acute; competition for their attention is lower; and a concentrated win is easier to demonstrate. The bet is that acute pain + low competition > brand prestige of Tier-1.

## Assumptions
- [[A-referral-currency-exists]] (relevant if pilot includes referral growth)
- Tier-2 recruiters value verification enough to pay (untested).

## Evidence (with provenance)
- Thin. This is exactly a *low-evidence* decision — per [[DNA-Decision-Principles]] DP3, buy information cheaply first → hence the attached experiment rather than a full commitment.

## Alternatives considered (and why rejected)
- **Tier-1 first** — higher prestige/signal, but crowded and verification is a weaker sell to already-credentialed students.
- **Both simultaneously** — splits scarce runway; rejected.

## Strategic DNA check
- Consistent with [[DNA-Non-Negotiables]]? ✅ (no trust/integrity conflict). Consistent with DP1 (reversible experiment) ✅.

## What would change my mind
- Pilot leading indicator (recruiter interest) below failure condition → reopen [[Q-tier2-before-tier1]].
