# 🏛 Airbnb

> One-line thesis: **A marketplace is won by obsessively fixing the un-scalable details of trust and quality early — then systematizing them.**

## Timeline (inflection points)
- 2008 — Founded (air mattresses + breakfast); repeatedly near-death, famously funding runway by selling cereal boxes.
- 2009 — Y Combinator (W09). Paul Graham's advice: **"Do things that don't scale."**
- 2009 — Diagnosed weak bookings → **terrible listing photos**; the founders went to NYC and manually re-shot listings (see [[Airbnb-photography-experiment]]). Weekly revenue roughly doubled.
- 2010s → — Scaled the manual insight into a free professional-photography program (thousands of photographers), then a global platform.

## Key strategic decisions
- **Manual quality intervention before automation** — do the un-scalable thing to learn what actually moves conversion.
- **Design/trust as the product** — in a stranger-to-stranger marketplace, *trust signals* (photos, reviews, profiles) are the core, not a feature.
- **Systematize the hack** once proven.

## Failures & near-death
Multiple near-deaths pre-YC; rejected by many investors; survived on grit and cereal. Cautionary and inspiring: survival ([[MM-margin-of-safety]]) bought the time to find the insight.

## Decision patterns
- Talk to users, fix the specific broken thing, measure ([[F-lean-startup]]).
- Invest in trust where the marketplace is thinnest ([[MM-marketplace-cold-start]]).

## Lessons for Acredemia
Trust artifacts *are* the product in a two-sided market. Early on, do the un-scalable thing (hand-verify, hand-hold recruiters) to learn what builds trust — then systematize. Quality of the verified profile drives recruiter conversion the way photos drove bookings.
