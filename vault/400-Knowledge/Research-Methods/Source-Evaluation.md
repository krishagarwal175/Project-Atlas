# 🧰 Source Evaluation & Evidence Quality

> Not all evidence is equal. A curated knowledge base is only as good as its willingness to *weight* sources by quality and record provenance. Garbage in, confident garbage out.

## What it is
A discipline for judging how much a piece of evidence should move your belief. A rough hierarchy (strongest → weakest): your own controlled experiment on your own users → a primary source (filings, first-party data) → a rigorous study → a credible case study with numbers → expert opinion → a blog narrative → a single anecdote → a vibe. Weight accordingly in the [[Vault-Governance|Evidence Ledger]].

## When to use it
Every time you add a supporting/contradicting entry to a theory, a market signal, or a case study. Especially market signals from the wild.

## How to run it (checklist)
1. **Primary or secondary?** 2. **What's the sample / n?** 3. **Survivorship?** (are we only seeing winners — [[MM-cognitive-biases]]). 4. **Incentive of the source** (who benefits from you believing it). 5. **Recency** (has the world changed — freshness/decay). 6. **Replication** (one study or many).

## Why it works
Provenance-weighting is the antidote to the narrative fallacy — it stops a compelling story from outvoting a strong dataset.

## Limitations / when NOT to use it
- Perfect evidence rarely exists early; the goal is *appropriate confidence*, not paralysis.
- Over-discounting qualitative signal (customer interviews) is its own error.

## Applies to Acredemia
Every case study carries `source-urls`; unsourced metrics are marked `[unverified]`. The [[Vault-Governance|governance bot]] flags claims without evidence, and confidence is *derived* from the ledger — never asserted.
