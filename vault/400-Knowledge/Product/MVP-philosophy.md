# 🧠 MVP Philosophy

> The Minimum Viable Product is the *smallest thing that produces a real learning signal about a specific hypothesis* — not "version 1 with fewer features." The purpose is learning, not shipping.

## The idea
An MVP tests the riskiest assumption with the least build. Often it isn't a product at all: a landing page (demand), a concierge/manual back-end (will they use it), a wizard-of-oz (fake the automation). "Viable" means *viable as an experiment* — it must be able to disprove the belief.

## When to reach for it
Before building anything expensive whose value is uncertain. The build step of [[F-lean-startup]].

## Why it works
The dominant startup cost is building the wrong thing; the MVP minimizes time-to-learning, not features.

## Failure modes / where it misleads
- **"MVP" as an excuse for a bad product** shipped to real users who churn and poison your data.
- **Testing the wrong assumption** — a beautiful MVP of a risk that wasn't the risk.
- **Feature creep** disguised as "minimum" — if you can't state the one hypothesis, it's not an MVP.

## Applies to Acredemia
[[EXP-2026-001-tier2-pilot]] is the model: smallest real test of the beachhead thesis with an armed failure condition. Never ship an MVP that risks the [[DNA-Non-Negotiables|non-negotiable]] of student trust just to move fast.
