# ⚔️ Contradiction search: two-sided product-native incentives

## Theory under attack
[[T-two-sided-incentives-drive-referral-virality]] — claim: **product-native** **two-sided** rewards drive viral referral *more reliably than* cash/one-sided.

## Counter-hypotheses searched
- Cash-reward programs that went viral anyway.
- One-sided programs that succeeded.
- Two-sided product-native programs that failed.

## What we found
| date | source | finding | breaks it? |
|------|--------|---------|-----------|
| 2026-07-09 | [[PayPal-referral]] | cash, two-sided → explosive viral growth | breaks the "product-native" half |
| 2026-07-09 | [[Dropbox-referral]] | product-native, two-sided → also worked | supports both halves |

## Verdict
- **outcome: broken (partial).** The **"product-native" requirement is refuted** — PayPal shows cash works when the referred user has immediate standalone utility. The **"two-sided" half survives** (both cases are two-sided; no counterexample found).
- **Actions taken on target theory:**
  1. Split the claim — demote "product-native" to a *boundary condition*, not a requirement.
  2. Set `contested: true` on the original combined claim; reframe the theory around the surviving "two-sided" core.
  3. Real driver appears to be **"the referred user must have standalone reason to use the product"** (Dropbox: storage; PayPal: payments) — promote *that* to the central claim.
- New insight worth a note: reward *type* (cash vs product) matters less than **referred-user standalone utility** + **funding to sustain any cash burn** (see PayPal caveat).
