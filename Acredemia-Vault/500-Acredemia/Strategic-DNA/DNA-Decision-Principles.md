# 🧬 Decision Principles

> **DRAFT for founder review.** Reusable heuristics for *how* Acredemia decides — consulted alongside evidence.

- **DP1 — Reversible over irreversible.** Prefer bets we can unwind; save irreversibility for high-conviction, well-tested calls.
- **DP2 — Optimize decision quality, not outcome luck.** Judge the reasoning, not the result (see [[Vault-Governance]]). A good process with a bad outcome is still a good decision.
- **DP3 — When evidence is thin, buy information cheaply first.** Run the smallest experiment that would change your mind before committing budget.
- **DP4 — Name the assumption you're most afraid of.** Every big decision surfaces its most fragile assumption and stress-tests it (sensitivity).
- **DP5 — Trust decays; re-check before reusing.** An old decision's evidence may be stale — confirm freshness before leaning on it again.
- **DP6 — Seek the counterexample.** Before committing to a belief, spend real effort trying to break it.
