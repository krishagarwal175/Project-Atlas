# 🧠 Growth Loops

> Sustainable growth comes from *loops*, not funnels. A loop reinvests its output back into its input: users → action → output that brings in more users. Funnels leak and need constant refilling; loops compound.

## The idea
A funnel is linear (spend → acquire → convert → done). A **loop** closes: e.g. new user creates content → content is discovered → brings a new user. The classic types: **viral/referral** (users invite users — [[Dropbox-referral]], [[PayPal-referral]]), **content** (users/UGC generate discoverable pages), **paid** (revenue funds more acquisition — only a loop if unit economics work, [[F-unit-economics]]).

## When to reach for it
Designing acquisition strategy; deciding whether a channel compounds or just spends. Ask "what does each new user *produce* that brings the next one?"

## Why it works
Loops reinvest output into input, so growth rate depends on prior growth — compounding ([[MM-systems-thinking]] reinforcing loop). Funnels have no memory.

## Failure modes / where it misleads
- **A "loop" that doesn't close** is just a funnel with a story.
- **Referral without referred-user standalone utility** brings dead signups ([[T-two-sided-incentives-drive-referral-virality]] boundary condition).
- Paid "loops" with negative unit economics accelerate death ([[Groupon-discount-growth]]).

## Applies to Acredemia
Candidate loop: verified student shares profile with a recruiter → recruiter sees value → invites more candidates / other recruiters → more reason for students to verify. Test whether it actually *closes* before investing ([[A-referral-currency-exists]]).
