# 📄 PayPal — Cash Referral

## Problem
PayPal needed to acquire a critical mass of users fast in a two-sided payments market with no organic distribution.

## Strategy
Direct **cash** referral: give new users ~$10 and the referrer ~$10 for a successful invite. Two-sided, but the reward was **money, not a product-native good.**

## Execution
Paid signup bonuses funded by venture capital, deliberately burning money to buy the network; tapered as the network reached self-sustaining density (notably seeded on eBay power-sellers).

## Outcome (metrics)
Explosive early growth to millions of users; reportedly ~$60–70M spent on the bonus program before tapering. Considered a canonical growth-hack success.

## Why it worked
- Two-sided → both parties motivated.
- Cash is universally desired → no dependence on a product-native currency.
- Worked because a payments product has **immediate transactional utility** for the referred user (they had a reason to actually use it).

## Transferability to Acredemia
This is a **counterexample to the "product-native" half** of [[T-two-sided-incentives-drive-referral-virality]] — cash worked fine. Reinforces the "two-sided" half. Feeds [[CON-cash-referrals-that-worked]]. Caveat: it required deep VC funding to sustain the burn, which Acredemia lacks — so "cash works" ≠ "cash is available to us."
