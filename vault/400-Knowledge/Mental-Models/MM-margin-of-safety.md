# 🧠 Margin of Safety & Avoiding Ruin

> Survive first. No expected-value calculation matters if a bad draw ends the game. Build slack so that being wrong is survivable.

## The idea
Some losses are terminal — they hit an **absorbing barrier** you can't come back from (bankruptcy, lost trust, a legal breach). For a startup, the barrier is **running out of runway**. A positive-EV bet that risks the barrier is still wrong, because you don't get to play the long run if you're eliminated. Margin of safety means sizing bets so the worst plausible case leaves you alive and able to keep making decisions.

## When to reach for it
Any irreversible or bet-the-company decision; fundraising timing; aggressive spend; anything with tail risk.

## Why it works
Compounding requires survival. The math of ruin is asymmetric: a 50% loss needs a 100% gain to recover. Non-ergodic systems (you only get one life) reward caution the ensemble average hides.

## Failure modes / where it misleads
- **Excessive caution** starves growth; margin of safety bounds the downside, it doesn't forbid risk.
- Confusing *volatility* (survivable) with *ruin* (terminal). Only guard hard against ruin.

## Applies to Acredemia
Two absorbing barriers: **runway** and **student trust** ([[DNA-Non-Negotiables]]). Every big bet is checked against both. Prefer reversible experiments ([[DNA-Decision-Principles]] DP1) so mistakes are survivable.
