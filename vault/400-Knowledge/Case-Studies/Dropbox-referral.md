# 📄 Dropbox — Referral Program

## Problem
Paid acquisition (search ads) cost more per signup than Dropbox's product could justify for a freemium storage tool.

## Strategy
Two-sided referral: **both** referrer and referred friend got extra free storage — the reward was the *product itself*, not cash.

## Execution
Built the referral flow directly into onboarding; made inviting friends a natural next step; reward was instant and visible.

## Outcome (metrics)
Widely reported ~3900% signup growth over ~15 months; referrals drove a large share of signups at near-zero marginal cost.

## Why it worked
- Reward was in the product's own currency (storage) → aligned incentive, no cash burn.
- Two-sided → both parties benefit, reducing social friction of inviting.
- Value of the reward *increased* the more you used the product.

## Transferability to Acredemia
Directional only. Boundary condition: works when the reward is a *product-native* good the user already wants. For Acredemia, "extra free storage" has no analog — the open question is whether a verification/hiring product *has* a product-native referral currency at all.
