# 📄 Slack — Bottom-up Adoption

## Problem
Selling collaboration software top-down (to IT/execs) is slow and high-friction.

## Strategy
Let individual teams adopt Slack for free, deliver value inside one team, then expand organically across the org until a company-wide purchase became inevitable.

## Execution
Generous free tier; frictionless team creation; the product itself demonstrated value before any sales conversation; pricing kicked in at scale.

## Outcome (metrics)
One of the fastest-growing SaaS products of its era; land-and-expand became the reference GTM motion for B2B SaaS.

## Why it worked
- Value was visible **within a single team** before any purchase decision.
- Expansion was driven by users, not sales.
- Requires a product whose value shows up fast at small scale.
