# 👥 User Personas — the three sides

> Framed as **jobs to be done** ([[F-jtbd]]), not demographics. Draft for founder review — refine with real [[Hypothesis-and-Experiments|user research]].

## 1. The Student / Early-career candidate (supply)
- **Job:** "Prove my ability and experience to a recruiter so I'm trusted without needing a brand-name degree."
- **Pain:** credibility gap, especially outside Tier-1 institutions ([[Q-tier2-before-tier1]]); résumés are unverifiable and easy to inflate.
- **What "value" looks like:** getting *seen and trusted* by real recruiters. Standalone value even before recruiters arrive is the cold-start key ([[MM-marketplace-cold-start]]).
- **Non-negotiable:** must never be paywalled from proving basic truth about themselves ([[DNA-Non-Negotiables]]).

## 2. The Recruiter / Employer (demand, the payer)
- **Job:** "Find early-career candidates I can *trust quickly* without a background-check tax."
- **Pain:** noise, fraud risk, slow verification, cost per hire.
- **Value:** trusted signal → faster, cheaper, lower-risk hiring. This side carries monetization ([[F-unit-economics]]).
- **Willingness-to-pay is the critical unknown** — [[Q-recruiters-first-or-students-first]].

## 3. The College / Institution (distribution + authority)
- **Job:** "Improve our students' placement outcomes and our reputation."
- **Role:** a distribution channel and an authoritative verification source; a beachhead for density ([[MM-network-effects]]).

## Sequencing question
Which side is the "hard side"? Almost certainly **verified-student supply**. See [[Marketplace-Dynamics]].
