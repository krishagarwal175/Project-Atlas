# 🏢 Competitive Landscape — verified hiring

> Framed by the **job** ([[F-jtbd]]), competitors are anything a student/recruiter "hires" to solve trust in early-career hiring — not just companies in our category. Draft; each competitor should become its own `competitor` profile with sourced facts.

## The alternatives (by category)
- **Professional networks (LinkedIn):** incumbent for candidate discovery; weak on *verified* ability. Strength: distribution + network effects. Threat: could add verification (entrant threat, [[F-porters-five-forces]]).
- **Background-check / verification vendors:** verify credentials but are slow, employer-paid, one-shot, not a two-sided network. Substitute for the *verification* job, not the *discovery* job.
- **Campus placement portals / college T&P cells:** own the student relationship in India; fragmented, offline, low trust-signal.
- **Skill-assessment platforms (HackerRank, etc.):** verify a narrow skill; not identity/experience trust.
- **"Doing nothing" / the résumé + interview status quo:** the real default. Cheap, universal, and low-trust — the incumbent to beat.

## Where the gap is
No one owns **trusted, two-sided, student-owned verified history** with [[MM-network-effects|network effects]]. That's the wedge.

## The honest risks
- An incumbent (LinkedIn) bolts on verification and wins on distribution.
- Trust is hard to build and easy to lose ([[DNA-Non-Negotiables]]).
