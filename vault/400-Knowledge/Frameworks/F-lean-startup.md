# 🧰 Lean Startup — Build–Measure–Learn

> A startup is an engine for *validated learning* under uncertainty. Turn beliefs into falsifiable experiments, run the smallest test that could change your mind, and let evidence — not opinion — drive the next move.

## What it is
The loop: **Build** the minimum needed to test a hypothesis → **Measure** a real behavioral signal → **Learn** whether to persevere or pivot. Minimize the *time* through the loop, not the effort spent building.

## When to use it
Whenever you're uncertain whether customers want it, will pay, or will retain — i.e. almost everything pre-PMF. This is the engine behind Atlas's [[TPL-experiment|Experiment Planner]].

## How to run it
1. State the riskiest assumption as a falsifiable hypothesis. 2. Define the metric and the *failure condition* up front. 3. Build the smallest thing that tests it (concierge, landing page, manual). 4. Decide *before* seeing data what result means persevere vs pivot.

## Why it works
It attacks the biggest cost in a startup — building the wrong thing — by buying information cheaply before committing ([[MM-expected-value]]: information can be +EV).

## Limitations / when NOT to use it
- **Local maxima.** Endless A/B optimization can't find a step-change; pair with [[MM-first-principles]] / vision.
- **Vanity metrics** fake learning; measure behavior, not applause ([[F-okrs-north-star]]).
- Not everything is testable cheaply (brand, some deep tech).

## Applies to Acredemia
Every strategic bet becomes a cheap experiment with an armed failure condition ([[EXP-2026-001-tier2-pilot]] is the model). "Buy information before committing" is [[DNA-Decision-Principles]] DP3.
