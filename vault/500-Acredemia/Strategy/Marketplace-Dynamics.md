# ⚖️ Acredemia Marketplace Dynamics

> How the two-sided (three-sided) market actually works, and where it can break. Draft for founder review. Applies the general theory ([[MM-network-effects]], [[MM-marketplace-cold-start]], [[P-marketplace-leakage-kills-unit-economics]]) to us specifically.

## The core loop (the prize)
Verified students → recruiter trust → recruiter demand & engagement → more reason for students to verify → more supply. A reinforcing loop ([[MM-systems-thinking]]) — the intended moat.

## The hard side
Likely **verified-student supply**: verification takes effort and the value is only realized once recruiters engage. Strategy: give students **standalone value** before recruiters arrive (a portable, owned, verified record) and win **density in a beachhead** (colleges) before breadth.

## The balancing loops that can kill it (design against these)
- **Leakage / disintermediation:** once a recruiter trusts a candidate, do they go off-platform? We need *ongoing* value post-match ([[P-marketplace-leakage-kills-unit-economics]] — the Homejoy failure).
- **Trust decay:** one faked verification poisons the signal ([[DNA-Non-Negotiables]] NN1). Trust is the product; guard it hard.
- **Discount-acquired demand:** subsidizing recruiters who don't retain ([[Groupon-discount-growth]]).

## Monetization structure
Students free (trust flywheel, [[Q-should-verification-stay-free]]); recruiter side pays. Both sides' CAC must be carried by recruiter LTV — model before scaling ([[F-unit-economics]]).
